# Val Geisler — LinkedIn Post
**Date:** April 2026
**Source:** https://linkedin.com/in/valgeisler

I spent 15 years in retention and lifecycle before coming to Digioh.

Freelancing. Time at Klaviyo. Leading retention brand-side. I built the flows, obsessed over segmentation, and sent emails that looked like nothing in preview because they were entirely dynamic.

Different copy, different imagery, different offers. All for different people.

And it worked.

Higher conversions, higher LTV, and stronger engagement.

Because it felt relevant.

But here’s what always frustrated me:

We couldn’t extend that same experience to the website. A beautifully personalized email would drive the click… and that subscriber would land on the same generic homepage as everyone else.

The same popup, the same discount, the same message.

Existing subscribers saw “10% off when you join our email list.”

Recent customers got the same welcome offer as first-time visitors.

For years, that disconnect felt normal. Email was smart while the website reset every session.

Cookies expired. Devices changed. The onsite experience forgot, even though the CRM remembered everything.

And that’s when I realized: most teams don’t actually have a personalization problem.

They have a continuity problem.

We’ve optimized the inbox to feel one-to-one. Then we send people to a one-to-many experience.

When someone clicks from an email, the site should pick up the conversation, not start over.

That might mean:
Not asking for an email address you already have
Not leading with a discount for someone who always buys at full price
Not re-explaining a product they’ve already purchased
Not hiding subscription or loyalty options from your best customers

This isn’t personalization for the sake of it. It’s respecting the relationship you’ve already built.

Email taught us that relevance converts.

Now it’s time for the storefront to hold up its end of the bargain
